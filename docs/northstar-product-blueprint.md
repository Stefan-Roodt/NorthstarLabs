# NorthstarLabs product blueprint

## The product thesis

NorthstarLabs should not compete as another place to upload videos.

The strongest course platforms already combine courses, coaching, memberships, communities, live events, commerce, and analytics. The unresolved problem is helping a person move from an intended outcome to the right combination of structure and human support, then keeping the journey connected.

NorthstarLabs should own this promise:

> Tell us where you want to go. NorthstarLabs helps you find the course, coach, and next action that can credibly move you forward.

The memory system is:

- Learn: get a structured path.
- Ask: add credible human support at the roadblock.
- Progress: keep the work, feedback, milestones, and proof connected.

## What the best products teach us

### Course businesses

Teachable, Thinkific, and Kajabi make it increasingly easy to create and sell courses, coaching, downloads, memberships, and communities. Their strength is creator commerce and broad product packaging.

Northstar should retain:

- Fast course creation and publishing.
- Flexible content types and protected media.
- Products, bundles, memberships, and recurring access.
- Branded storefronts and clear creator analytics.

Northstar should improve:

- Begin with the learner's intended outcome, not the catalogue hierarchy.
- Connect a course to a discoverable external coach marketplace.
- Use support signals to improve both the learner path and creator curriculum.

### Community and cohort learning

Circle is strong at keeping community, events, live rooms, and course content together. Maven emphasizes projects, cohort accountability, and tangible work.

Northstar should retain:

- Course-linked conversations rather than a generic social feed.
- Live sessions, office hours, and cohort touchpoints.
- Projects that produce useful evidence, not only content consumption.

Northstar should improve:

- Trigger human support when a learner reports a roadblock.
- Connect each conversation or live session to a goal and next action.
- Make progress visible as useful output, not only percentage watched.

### Expert marketplaces

Preply demonstrates the value of clear prices, trial experiences, verified identity and qualifications, availability, response speed, attendance, conversion, and learner reviews.

Northstar should retain:

- Search by need.
- Transparent self-set hourly rates.
- Verified credentials separated from paid placement.
- Real availability, protected enquiries, and completed-session reviews.
- Reliability signals that are earned rather than bought.

Northstar should improve:

- Let a learner bring course context into a coaching request.
- Recommend a coach for a specific roadblock rather than a broad category alone.
- Return the agreed next actions to the learner's progress record.

## The differentiated system

### 1. Goal graph

Every learner begins with an outcome:

- Intended result.
- Current starting point.
- Preferred support style.
- Timing and constraints.
- Evidence that would prove progress.

This becomes a reusable goal record rather than a disposable search phrase.

### 2. Credible matching

The matching layer ranks:

- Topic and semantic outcome fit.
- Starting-level fit.
- Delivery preference.
- Time and price fit.
- Creator or coach evidence.
- Availability and reliability.
- Verified learner outcomes.

Paid visibility can affect placement only when clearly labelled. It must never change verification or trust scores.

### 3. Blended path

A recommended path can contain:

- A short course or selected lessons.
- One diagnostic or project.
- A coach session for a specific roadblock.
- A live session or small peer group.
- A milestone and a piece of evidence.

The learner sees one next action, not five disconnected products.

### 4. Progress loop

After every useful activity, ask:

- What changed?
- What remains unclear?
- What is the next action?
- Is human help now valuable?
- What evidence was created?

This loop feeds the learner dashboard, creator insights, and coach follow-up without exposing private notes.

### 5. Trust layer

Trust must remain legible:

- Identity verification.
- Credential review.
- Verified-session reviews.
- Two-way ratings protected by blind reveal and minimum aggregation.
- Response, attendance, and cancellation reliability.
- Clear separation of sponsored placement and earned trust.
- Dispute and report workflows.

### 6. Creator intelligence

Creators should see:

- Where learners stop.
- Which outcomes bring people to the course.
- Common roadblocks and help requests.
- Which lessons precede coach enquiries.
- Which projects demonstrate progress.
- Which gaps should become a lesson, live session, or new course.

This should make NorthstarLabs more useful with every learner interaction.

## Experience principles

1. Ask for a goal before asking for an account.
2. Show a useful result before asking for payment.
3. Never manufacture a weak match to make a catalogue look full.
4. Explain why a course or coach is recommended.
5. Let people inspect price, proof, availability, and structure early.
6. Keep paid placement visibly separate from trust.
7. Give every screen one obvious next action.
8. Measure completed outcomes and useful evidence, not just clicks or watch time.
9. Make all important identity, brand, and catalogue fields self-service.
10. Keep NorthstarLabs memorable: Learn. Ask. Progress.

## Delivery sequence

### Foundation now

- Goal-first Northstar Navigator.
- Searchable courses and coaches from the same goal.
- Honest no-match state.
- Detailed request capture and administrator response queue.
- Connected course, coaching, community, live, progress, and rating infrastructure.

### Next release

- Persist learner goals after signup.
- Add structured outcomes and levels to courses and coach profiles.
- Introduce an explainable matching score.
- Let learners save a recommended route.
- Add a course-to-coach handoff with lesson and roadblock context.

### Growth release

- Coach trial or discovery session workflow.
- Reliability metrics: response time, attendance, rescheduling, completed sessions.
- Project submissions and outcome portfolios.
- Goal-based cohorts and office hours.
- Creator gap reports generated from anonymized demand patterns.

### Intelligence release

- Goal graph spanning courses, coaches, live sessions, and projects.
- At-risk progress signals with helpful, non-spam interventions.
- Personalized next-action recommendations.
- Outcome and evidence dashboards for learners, creators, and organizations.

## Metrics that matter

Primary product metric:

- Meaningful progress events per active learner per month.

Supporting metrics:

- Goal-to-credible-match rate.
- Time from first visit to useful next action.
- Course-to-coach handoff rate when a roadblock is reported.
- Saved-path activation.
- Project or milestone completion.
- Verified-session satisfaction.
- Learner return after receiving human support.
- Creator response to unmet demand.

Avoid treating signups, page views, or video completion as sufficient evidence of value.
